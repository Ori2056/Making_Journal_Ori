### Reflective Proposal

1) Review and Reflect


I most enjoyed the data portrait experiments. I found it really interesting to be thinking reflexively about my daily subconscious practices, especially when it came to discussing the things I enjoy such as music. I found it interesting to do my own study on coding, as well as being able to later use vibe coding to fill in the gaps and explore new possibilities that my limited knowledge of coding couldn’t overcome, such as creating a variety of shapes which had varied movements, speeds and interactive aspects. 


Connecting it to the readings I have to do for Des300, I find that the cyclical nature of the design process especially when it comes to coding is very heavily introspective, and allows me to immediately see where I am struggling and where I am having success in my design work. I think that the iterative nature of the vibe coding also allowed me to develop my knowledge of the code by reviewing it after each prompt, and seeing what changes were made to the code and how they influenced the result. 


I think it would be quite interesting to try exploring data humanism further, as I think it is important to explore the deep relationships between data points and the everydays of human life and the human experience as a whole. I think it will also give me room to be more reflective as I hope to analyse the experiences of myself and others within this framework.


I think my work mostly aligns with the ideas of interactivity that we discussed in class, mostly focusing on the ways that interactivity can be approached within a data design framework.


I think I would like to continue working on interactivity, thinking about how I can utilise multi-modality within the digital landscape and potentially looking into the accessibility factors associated with it such as proximity, disability and access.


2) Thematic Focus and Data Source(s)


I think I would like to focus on some kind of interpersonal connection for my design project. Maybe something like gauging when people come together and bond over a shared interest or experience.


It could be interesting to examine this through something like a data pool of the thoughts and sensations that arise from this that is recontextualised as non-static data and then transformed into a visualised output.


The data is live, and could be best interpreted from social media, text forums or other forms of text based, live, online content. 


The data will be turned into some form of constantly evolving web, maybe a play on the concept of a “social network” through direct visualisation? I think this would allow a level of transparency between people (of course taking into account the ethical considerations that would come in reaction to having public and live data revealing personal information from the voluntary participants).


I hope that this project will bridge the gaps in social technology where people feel limited through contact over devices, as it will be more akin to how people react and engage with one another in real life. It will also hopefully be able to project and create insights for people about how they may navigate social circumstances both online and in real life (though not totally inform it as that has potentially poor ethical ramifications).
3) Visualisation & Impact
I thought of this project with the digital era in mind. The young generations are becoming more socially isolated due to an increased appeal in online social engagement versus offline, and I would like to crumble the manufactured “comfort” that is so attractive about the online space, so that people can hopefully feel more incentivised to build interpersonal relationships and connections outside the internet.
